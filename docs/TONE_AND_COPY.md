# Tone and Microcopy Guidelines - Pick Your Linux

This guide defines the voice for all user-facing text.
The goal is to be friendly, reassuring, and clear for non-technical users.

---

## Voice Principles
- Friendly and calm.
- Practical, not salesy.
- Short sentences. One idea per line.
- Focus on what the user wants, not what the system needs.

---

## Do and Don't

Do
- Use plain language.
- Explain choices without jargon.
- Make options feel safe and reversible.

Don't
- Use technical terms or acronyms.
- Label the user by skill level.
- Imply a single "best" answer.

---

## Forbidden Language

Disallowed terms
- CLI, terminal, drivers, codecs, architecture
- beginner, advanced, expert
- best, recommended, popular

Disallowed concepts
- Skill labels
- Subjective rankings
- Popularity or community votes

---

## Good vs Bad Examples

Bad: "Are you a beginner or advanced user?"
Good: "If something doesn't work, what feels right to you?"

Bad: "Do you want the best distro?"
Good: "What do you mainly want to use this computer for?"

Bad: "Do you want proprietary drivers?"
Good: "How important is it that everything works right away?"

---

## Guidance for Future Questions

- Start with the user's goal.
- Avoid hidden implications (no judgement, no pressure).
- Map each answer to one or more intent fields.
- Keep answer options short and specific.

---

## UI Label Guidance

Preferred
- "Continue"
- "Back"
- "See results"
- "Start over"

Avoid
- "Advanced options"
- "Expert mode"
- "Best choice"
